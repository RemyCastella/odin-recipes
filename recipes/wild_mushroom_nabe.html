Wild Mushroom Nabe (Japanese Hotpot)

Description

The chorus of nightime crickets and refreshing breeze remind us that summer is coming to an end, ushering in the nostalgic, cozy and bountiful autumn.

One of my favorite autumn memories is when I went with my girlfriend to buy wild mushrooms from a farmer's market up in the Hokkaido mountains to preparing a wild mushroom nabe on our camping trip. We enjoyed piping hot bowl after piping hot bowl as we sat under the stars, warmed up by the campfire and the wild mushroom delight.

Now, whenever I notice the first signs of autumn, I know the perfect time has come for some hearty wild mushroom nabe!

Ingredients

  • Chicken thigh (300g)
  • Wild mushrooms of choice (400g)

    • Example: enoki, maitake, shimeji, nameko, etc.

  • Leek (half)
  • Thinly sliced ginger (1 piece)
  • Dashi broth (3 cups)
  • Soy sauce (2 tbsp)
  • Sake (2 tbsp)
  • Mirin (1 tbsp)

Steps

  1. Cut the hard bases from the mushrooms and separate the mushrooms by hand
  2. Thinly slice the leek diagonally
  3. Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces
  4. Place the chicken, dash broth, soy sauce, sake, and mirin into an earthenware pot
  5. Place the pot on medium-high heat and simmer with the lid on for around 5 minutes
  6. Add the leek and mushrooms and simmer for another 3 minutes
